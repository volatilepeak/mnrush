Our Program
Built on Development.
Driven by Competition.

Founded in 2017, Minnesota Rush is focused on skill development and competitive excellence. Our coaches help young athletes achieve their dreams in basketball and beyond.

01

Skill Development

Focused training tailored to each athlete's strengths and growth areas. We build technical skills, basketball IQ, and physical conditioning.

02

Exposure & Competition

Top-tier opportunities on the Prep Hoops Circuit. We showcase talent and cultivate an atmosphere of intense, meaningful competition.

03

Experienced Coaching

Coaches who mentor and inspire — many coach high school basketball and bring deep expertise and understanding of the athlete's journey.

Why Rush
This Isn't Your
Typical AAU Program.
"We built this program because developing players fundamentally, building IQ, and fostering real competition matters more than anything. Too much of AAU basketball has become about shortcuts and exposure over actual development. We don't buy into that. 4th–9th grade basketball should be about growing as an individual player, growing in a team setting with different teammates, and building a genuine love for the game — while competing hard every single time out."
— Minnesota Rush Coaching Staff

Effort Over Talent

We outwork opponents regardless of size, rankings, or hype.

Team > Individual

Stats never outweigh winning plays. Selfless basketball, always.

Growth Mindset

We teach, correct, and develop. Mistakes are part of progress.

Accountability

Players and coaches hold each other to standards. No exceptions.

How We Play

High Tempo Relentless Defense Smart Shot Selection Player-First Development Organized Systems Toughness

What We Build

Tough Competitors Smart Decision-Makers Confident Shot-Takers High-IQ Players

Our Coaching Standard

Teacher First, Coach Second Correct Loudly, Praise Louder Development > Short-Term Wins No Gimmicks

Leadership
Program Directors
Boys Program Director
Trevor Walton

A 2010 graduate and former player at Maple Grove High School, Trevor is the Varsity Assistant for Rogers Boys Basketball and former head coach of North Branch Boys Basketball.

Trevor realized the potential of a program in the Northwest Metro along I-94, sparking the creation of MN Rush in 2017.

boys@mnrush.com
Girls Program Director
James Bezanson

James took on the girls director role after coaching at the 13U and 14U levels. Previously a Varsity Assistant for North Branch Boys Basketball and currently the High Jump Coach for STMA Track & Field.

James identified the opportunity to build a high-quality girls' program in the Northwest Metro.
